About me
Experienced professional in financial and marketing analytics. Executed data-driven solutions to increase efficiency, accuracy, whilst utilizing internal data processing. I am a results driven, multi-lingual professional with a unique combination of broad and deep business plus technology understanding. Goal-oriented team leader with the capacity to create synergy and integrity in an organization.

Western healthcare, particularly in the United States, faces staggering challenges in the 21 st century. While technical advances increase exponentially, their costs do as well. Moreover, there is considerable uncertainty as to how to best utilize these resources and equitably, optimally distribute them. The most obvious challenge is the achievement of cost containment while simultaneously improving access. Elements of this problem include effecting preventative care, anticipating and managing epidemics, encouraging pure research and accelerating the assessment and implementation of new therapies. At the minimum, such goals require informed strategic planning, optimization of staffing and facility availability, the detection and reduction of healthcare fraud, and data-driven engagement with third-party payers.
Such questions are subject to statistical analysis; thus, my background in business intelligence and analytics has well-equipped me to investigate such issues objectively and offer the resulting insights to the various stakeholders within the healthcare system. Such insights are potentially applicable to both clinical practice and academic research, effecting cost-containment while improving access by revealing “best practice methods.” My interest in this field is both personal and professional. From a humanitarian standpoint, I would find it rewarding to contribute to the delivery of healthcare to others, even if I were doing so indirectly. Professionally, it’s difficult to imagine a more challenging or worthwhile field in which to engage my training.
